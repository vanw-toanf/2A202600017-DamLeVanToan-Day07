Customer Support Playbook for the AI Knowledge Assistant

The support team uses the knowledge assistant to answer repetitive questions about account setup, billing errors, password recovery, and service limitations. To make retrieval useful, the indexed documents should contain clear issue descriptions, step-by-step resolutions, escalation criteria, and references to the owning team.

When writing support content, authors should avoid vague statements such as "check the settings" or "contact engineering if needed." Instead, each article should specify the exact page, button, or log source that an agent should inspect. Specific language makes chunks more useful because the retriever can match concrete troubleshooting terms found in the user's question.

Metadata should distinguish between customer-facing articles, internal support-only notes, and engineering-only incident documents. If these sources are mixed together without filtering, the assistant may surface internal-only procedures to the wrong audience or retrieve engineering details when the user only needs a short customer-safe explanation.

A high-quality support assistant should also recognize when retrieval is insufficient. For example, if no document explains a newly introduced billing issue, the system should recommend escalation instead of improvising a risky answer. In practice, honest uncertainty is better than a polished but incorrect response.

To maintain reliability, the support operations team should review failed queries every week. Those reviews often reveal missing documents, poor chunk boundaries, or stale articles that still rank highly because their language overlaps with common queries.
